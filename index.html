WELCOME TO SOLUS AURORA

Geomagnetic storms are powerful disturbances in Earth's magnetic field caused by solar activity, particularly coronal mass ejections (CMEs). When charged particles from the Sun collide with Earth's magnetosphere, they create the stunning aurora borealis (Northern Lights). This website showcases data and analysis from the intense geomagnetic storms of May 2024, which generated some of the most vivid auroras seen worldwide. Our platform features an interactive aurora visibility heatmap, helping users explore the regions most affected.
